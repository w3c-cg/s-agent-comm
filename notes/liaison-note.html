This Liaison Note summarizes the scope, positioning, and non-normative outputs of the Semantic Agent Communication Community Group, with the intent to support information exchange with other W3C Groups and external standards communities.

This document is a Community Group Note. It is non-normative and does not constitute a W3C Recommendation. Discussion and feedback are tracked publicly via the Group’s GitHub repositories.

Scope and purpose

The Semantic Agent Communication Community Group develops semantic models and ontological structures that enable interoperable communication between autonomous software agents acting as delegated and accountable technical principals.

The scope of this work is limited to the semantic layer, including shared semantics for identity binding, capability description, intent expression, structured delegation, and verifiable execution records. The Group does not define transport protocols, execution runtimes, internal reasoning architectures, or hardware-specific mechanisms.

Relationship to existing W3C work

The Group’s work builds upon established Web and Semantic Web foundations, including RDF, Linked Data, Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs), and Verifiable Credentials (VCs). The Group aims to remain compatible with existing standards and does not seek to redefine or replace them.

Areas of potential alignment

Potential areas for future alignment or information exchange may include:

  • Semantic representations of agent identity and delegation
  • Capability and intent modeling across distributed systems
  • Structured execution records and accountability concepts
  • Interoperability considerations between agent systems and Web-based identity infrastructures

Any alignment activities are exploratory and non-binding.

Nature of outputs

Outputs of the Community Group are published as Community Group documents (Reports or Notes). These documents are non-normative. The purpose of these outputs is to share concepts, vocabularies, schemas, and design approaches that may inform future standardization efforts.
